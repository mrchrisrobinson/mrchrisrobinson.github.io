Sales Engineer Manifesto

I am a Sales Engineer, one of the most valuable and underutilized resources at any company. How is a Sales Engineer valuable?
This is obvious to anyone who has had the pleasure of working with a professional Sales Engineer;
by increasing win rates and sales velocity of your sales team,
by correctly articulating the value of your solution to the customer,
by working with the product team to develope features your customers will pay for,
and by ultimately raising the ASP (Average Selling Price) of your solution.

This lifecycle that I can bring to a company that has not implemted a Sales Engineer department has obvious value, but the value does not stop there.
Sales Engineers can help all of these departments:
Sales: Demo, Proof of Concept, Technical Q & A, Training, Sys Opps for Product/Accounting environments
Marketing: Conferences, Canned Demos, Webinars, SME White Papers, Thought Leadership, Answers to forum questions
Product Marketing: Relevant messaging, Business analysis on competitors
Channel: Enablement, Training
CS: Save escalations, Train “CS Engineer” for backup demo and upsell/cross sell demos
Product Management: Voice of the Prospect program (helps AEs know what messaging is working), Feature interaction and flows, Presales Customizations
All of these activities can be directly linked back to revenue generating activities.
Now do you see the value of a Sales Engineer?
